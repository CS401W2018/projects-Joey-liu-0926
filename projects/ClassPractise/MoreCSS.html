1.What is the difference between block tags and inline tags.

Block Tags: Take up the entire width of the parent container, meaning they start on a new line and push the following content down. They generally form larger structure elements like paragraphs or divisions.

Inline Tags: only take up the necessary width to fit their content, and they don't force other content onto new lines. Typically used for smaller pieces of content within a block element.

2.What is the margin, border, and padding?

Margin: The outer space between the element's border and surrounding elements. Used to control the spacing outside an element.

Border: The line or edge surrounding the element's padding and content. Borders can have various styles (solid, dashed, etc.), colors, and thicknesses.

Padding: The inner space between the element's content and its border. Used to create space inside the element, between the content and the border.

4.When would you use a figure tag?

When you need to associate an image, chart, or media with a caption that provides context.
