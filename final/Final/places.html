Chengdu People's Park is a well-known public park in the heart of Chengdu, Sichuan Province, China. Established in 1911, it is one of the oldest and most popular parks in the city. The park offers a mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and leisure activities, making it a favorite spot for locals and tourists alike.

    "reasons for recommendation"

  • Historical Significance
  • Natural Scenery
  • Cultural and Social Hub
  • Seasonal Highlights

Chnegdu Taikoo Li is a high-end, open-air shopping and lifestyle complex located in the heart of Chengdu, Sichuan Province, near the iconic Daci Temple. It seamlessly blends modern architecture with traditional Sichuan culture, offering a unique urban experience.

    "reasons for recommendation"

  • Luxury Shopping and Dining
  • Architectural Design
  • Cultural Landmarks
  • Leisure and Entertainment

Chengdu Kuanzhai Alley is a historic and cultural landmark in Chengdu, Sichuan Province, consisting of three parallel alleys: Kuan Alley (Wide Alley), Zhai Alley (Narrow Alley), and Jing Alley (Well Alley). Originally part of a Qing Dynasty residential area, it has been revitalized into a vibrant hub for tourism, dining, and cultural experiences.

    "reasons for recommendation"

  • Historical Significance
  • Cultural and Leisure Activities
  • Dining and Shopping
  • Architectural Charm

Chengdu Wenshu Monastery is a renowned Buddhist temple located in Chengdu, Sichuan Province. It is dedicated to Manjushri, the Bodhisattva of Wisdom, and is one of the most important and well-preserved ancient Buddhist sites in the region. Established during the Tang Dynasty, it has been a spiritual and cultural center for over 1,300 years.

    "Reasons for recommendation"

  • Historical and Religious Significance
  • Architectural Beauty
  • Cultural Experience
  • Teahouse and Garden

Chengdu Sanxingduiis an archaeological site and museum located in Guanghan, about 40 kilometers north of Chengdu, Sichuan Province. It is one of the most significant discoveries of ancient Chinese civilization, showcasing the mysterious culture of the Sanxingdui civilization, which dates back over 3,000 years to the Bronze Age.

    "Reasons for recommendation"

  • Historical Significance
  • Unique Artifacts
  • Sanxingdui Museum
  • Cultural Mysteries
